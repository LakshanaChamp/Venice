"WELCOME TO MY FAVORITE DESTINATION IN THE WORLD"

Venice,the capital of northern Italy's Veneto region, is built on more than 100 small islands in a lagoon in the Adriatic Sea. It has no roads, just canals - including the Grand Canal thoroughfare - lined with Renaissance and Gotjic palaces. The central square, Piazz San Marco, contains St. Mark's Basilica, which is tiled width Byzantine mosaics, and the Camanile bell tower offering views of the city's red roofs.

5 GREAT PLACES TO VISIT IN VENICE

  1. BASICICA DI SAN MARCO

    Basilica di San Marco is the most important church in the city and one of the most important monuments in Italy, for its opulent design, gold ground mosaics, and its statues as a symbol of Venetian wealth and power, from its 11th century on the building has been known by the nickname Chiesa d'Oro

  2. PALAZZ DUCALE

    Palzzo Ducale is a masterpiece of Gothic art and repository of innumerable art treasures, it features a number of different and intricate architectural details, dating from the 9th century.

  3. PONTE DEI SOSPIRI

    Ponte dei Sospiri is a barqoue bridge that once connected Palazzo Ducale to the prisons. The bridge of sighs is one Venice's most important sights.

  4. CANAL GRANDE

    Canal Grande is known as the most beautiful street in the world. The Canal Grand is the main waterway of Venice lined in both sides by an uninturrupted serie of places and masterpieces

  5. TEATRO LA FENICE

    Teatro La Venicw was twice estroyed by fire and twice restored to its former splendour. For centuries La Fenice has been Venice's principal stage for world-class opers, music, theater and ballet.
